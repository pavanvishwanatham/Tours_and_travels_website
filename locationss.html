Discover The Beautiful India

Mysuru

If you think the beauty of Mysore is restricted to magnificent palaces and other majestic buildings, then you probably have not heard of Brindavan Gardens, Mysore Zoo, Chamundi Hills, or Karanji Lake. These spectacular places, including a few sprawling gardens, and some stunning waterfalls & lakes, makes Mysore, one of the best tourist destinations in Karnataka. Mysore, which is also known as the cultural capital of Karnataka, is home to many opulent and royal palaces. It has several yoga centres and lush green sandalwood forests, that gave this city names like 'City of Palaces', 'Sandalwood City', and 'City of Yoga.'

Recently the city was declared the 'second cleanest city' in India by the Union Urban Development Authority. This makes it one of the most sought-after tourist destinations in Karnataka, visited by a large number of tourists every year. The rich cultural heritage and historical significance of this glorious city attracts tourists from around the world, who explore the splendor of Mysore city that includes prehistoric sites, palaces, monuments, temples, churches, zoo, sandalwood forests and well-manicured gardens.

Bengaluru

Bengaluru- a place where various cultures crosses paths and splendid past mingles with modern pleasures, is fondly known as 'The Garden City of India'. This joyful city has earned quite a few names such as 'Silicon Valley of India', 'Air Conditioned City', 'Pub Capital of India', and 'Pensioners' Paradise' all for its lush green landscapes, healthy climate, and modern conveniences. Often termed as 'techie's paradise', Bengaluru has plenty of things that are of great tourist interest like gardens, natural landscapes, palaces, temples, and museums. All these things together represent a wonderful blend of past and present, and this is the reason a lot of people admire the city for its westward culture, natural beauty & vibrant nightlife.

Bengaluru is a potpourri of people and culture and is definitely one of the best cities in India, that draws people in a large number from across the world. It is the capital of Karnataka and an important tourist destination, which is currently Asia's fastest growing cosmopolitan city that almost every techie would love visiting. The convenient location of Bengaluru in the center of Peninsular India, makes the atmosphere cool & pleasant, attracting hundreds of tourists to this modern town every year.

Pondicherry

PONDICHERRY TRAVEL GUIDE Oh the charm of the French world meeting the sea life - how can one not fall for something so surreal yet so real? The quietude of the alleys and the bustling boulevards; the amalgamation of Tamil and French cuisines and architecture; and the jaunty crowd, all make Pondicherry a compelling place to visit in India. The Union Territory of Pondicherry blessed with picturesque coastline is indeed a destination for beach lovers where they can enjoy multiple water activities. The colonial architecture compliments the beach life whereas the far situated Auroville add another dimension to Pondicherry tourism. If there is one tourist destination in India that can cater to your need for a relaxing holiday, Pondicherry has to be it.

Hyderabad

Hyderabad, often referred to as the "City of Pearls," is a vibrant and historic city located in the southern part of India. It serves as the capital of the Indian state of Telangana and is known for its rich cultural heritage, magnificent architecture, delectable cuisine, and warm hospitality.

Hyderabad boasts an impressive array of historical landmarks that showcase its glorious past. The iconic Charminar, a magnificent monument with four minarets, is a symbol of the city and a must-visit attraction. Golconda Fort, Qutub Shahi Tombs, Chowmahalla Palace, and Falaknuma Palace are other architectural marvels that offer glimpses into the city's regal history.

Munnar

Munnar, located in the state of Kerala, India, is a picturesque hill station renowned for its breathtaking beauty and serene ambiance. Nestled in the Western Ghats mountain range, Munnar is blessed with lush greenery, rolling hills, mist-covered valleys, and sprawling tea plantations. It is a popular destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and those seeking tranquility amidst nature's splendor.

One of the major attractions of Munnar is its tea estates. The region is known for its tea cultivation, and the tea gardens create a mesmerizing landscape of emerald-green plantations. Visitors can explore the tea estates, learn about the tea-making process, and even indulge in tea tasting sessions.

Kochi

Kochi, also known as Cochin, is a vibrant city located in the state of Kerala, India. It is a major port city on the southwestern coast of the country and is often referred to as the "Queen of the Arabian Sea." Kochi is a popular travel destination due to its rich history, diverse culture, stunning natural beauty, and thriving cosmopolitan atmosphere.

Kochi has a fascinating history that dates back to ancient times. It has been a significant trading hub for centuries, attracting merchants from various parts of the world. The city's historical landmarks, such as the Fort Kochi area, showcase a unique blend of Indian, European, and Asian architectural styles.

Kerala

Kerala, located in the southwestern part of India, is a state renowned for its breathtaking natural beauty, diverse cultural heritage, and unique travel experiences. Known as "God's Own Country," Kerala offers a captivating blend of serene backwaters, lush green landscapes, pristine beaches, mist-covered hill stations, and vibrant wildlife.

One of the most prominent attractions in Kerala is its extensive network of backwaters, consisting of interconnected lakes, lagoons, and canals. The iconic backwaters of Alappuzha, Kumarakom, and Kollam provide an enchanting experience as you cruise through the tranquil waters on traditional houseboats known as "kettuvallams." These houseboats offer a chance to witness the rustic life along the banks and indulge in delicious local cuisine.

hampi

Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the state of Karnataka, India. It is a captivating historical and archaeological site known for its ancient ruins, temples, and impressive architecture. Hampi was once the capital of the mighty Vijayanagara Empire during the 14th to 16th centuries.

The ruins of Hampi spread over a vast area of around 4,100 hectares and offer a glimpse into the glorious past of the Vijayanagara Empire. The site is characterized by an incredible fusion of Hindu and Islamic architectural styles, reflecting the cultural diversity of the empire.
